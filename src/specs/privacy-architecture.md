# ShieldVIN — Off-Chain PII Architecture
## GDPR / CCPA Compliance Design — Draft v0.2

**Status:** Draft — for review
**Date:** 2026-03

---

## 1. The Core Privacy Principle

The design rule is simple and non-negotiable: **no personally identifiable information (PII) is ever written to the Midnight Network blockchain**.

This is not just a GDPR compliance measure — it is foundational to the system's security model. A blockchain is immutable. If an owner's name, address, or government ID were ever written on-chain, it could never be deleted. GDPR's right to erasure (Article 17) and CCPA's right to deletion would be structurally impossible to satisfy.

ShieldVIN resolves this by design. The blockchain holds only:
- The VIN (vehicle identifier — not a personal identifier under GDPR)
- Technical hashes of vehicle hardware serials
- Ownership expressed as a cryptographic commitment (a hash — not a name)
- Vehicle status flags
- Event counts and timestamps

Owner identity is held exclusively by the off-chain systems of the relevant parties — the manufacturer at build, the dealer at point of sale, the insurer at policy inception, the government at registration. These parties already hold this data as part of their existing regulated processes.

## 2. Ownership Without Identity — The Commitment Model

The ownership model works as follows:

**At manufacture:**
- The manufacturer generates a random owner secret `o` and salt `s`
- The owner commitment is `H(o ∥ s)` where H is SHA-256 — no vehicle identifier is included in the hash
- The commitment is stored on-chain in the `ownershipHash` field
- `o` and `s` are stored off-chain in the manufacturer's secure registry until point of sale
- No owner name or contact detail is ever created at this stage (the first owner is the manufacturer before point of sale)

**At point of sale:**
- The new owner generates their own private secret `o` (using their TN-3 device credential or a wallet)
- The new owner commitment is `H(o)` (SHA-256 of the owner secret — see the Compact contract `transferOwnership` circuit)
- The dealer submits the new commitment to the VAP-1 ownership transfer endpoint
- The dealer's off-chain system records `{vin, buyer_id, commitment_salt, timestamp}` in their regulated records
- The blockchain sees only: old commitment replaced by new commitment, transfer count incremented by 1

**At verification:**
- An owner proves ownership by demonstrating knowledge of the preimage of their commitment
- This proof is generated by the TN-3 module (or owner wallet application)
- The ZK proof confirms "the caller knows the secret that hashes to the on-chain commitment" without revealing the secret

## 3. Data Residency by Party

| Data | Where Stored | Jurisdiction | Erasure Possible? |
|------|-------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Owner name, address, ID | Dealer / insurer / DMV records | Local jurisdiction | Yes — by those parties |
| Ownership commitment (hash) | Midnight Network | Distributed | No — but not PII |
| VIN | Midnight Network | Distributed | No — but not PII under GDPR |
| Vehicle hardware serials (hashed) | Midnight Network | Distributed | No — but not directly linkable to person |
| Transfer event timestamps | Midnight Network | Distributed | No — but no personal data attached |
| Ownership preimage (commitment secret) | Owner device / wallet | Owner-controlled | Yes — owner deletes their wallet |

## 4. GDPR Compliance Analysis

### 4.1 Is Any On-Chain Data Personal Data?

Under GDPR Article 4(1), personal data is "any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person."

| Field | Personal data? | Reasoning |
|-------|---------------|-----------|
| VIN | No | A VIN identifies a vehicle, not a person. It is not classed as personal data under GDPR unless combined with registration data held by a third party. |
| `ownershipHash` | No | A cryptographic commitment with no preimage disclosed. Not linkable to a person without the off-chain salt. |
| Hardware hashes | No | Hashes of physical component serials. No natural person can be identified from these. |
| Event timestamps | No | Timestamps alone do not identify a person. |
| Status flags | No | Vehicle status (ACTIVE/STOLEN/etc.) is not personal data. |

**Conclusion:** The on-chain data store contains no personal data as defined by GDPR Article 4(1). GDPR obligations do not apply to the Midnight Network ledger data itself.

### 4.2 Off-Chain Data (Held by Third Parties)

Off-chain PII (names, addresses, document numbers) is held by existing regulated entities — manufacturers, dealers, insurers, government registries. These entities are already Data Controllers under GDPR for this data in the context of their existing regulated activities. ShieldVIN does not change their existing obligations.

The ShieldVIN API (VAP-1) never receives or processes personal data. It receives VINs and role credentials; it returns vehicle status and hashes. No PII flows through the ShieldVIN infrastructure layer.

### 4.3 Right to Erasure (Article 17)

An owner's personal data is held by dealers, insurers, and government agencies — not by ShieldVIN. Erasure requests are directed to those parties under their existing GDPR obligations. The on-chain record remains (it is not personal data) but the off-chain linkage to the individual can be severed.

When an owner loses or destroys their wallet credential, the commitment secret is lost. The vehicle can no longer be proven to be owned by that individual via ShieldVIN. This is equivalent to destruction of a key — the on-chain commitment becomes an orphaned hash.

### 4.4 Data Protection by Design (Article 25)

ShieldVIN is compliant by construction:
- Minimum data: only what is technically necessary is on-chain
- Pseudonymisation: ownership expressed as a hash commitment
- No personal data on a shared ledger: personal data stays with existing regulated parties
- Access control: ZK proofs enforce role-based disclosure — no party can query data beyond their authorised scope

## 5. CCPA Compliance Analysis

Under CCPA, a "consumer" is a California resident. CCPA grants rights to know, delete, and opt-out regarding personal information.

The analysis follows the same structure as GDPR: the on-chain data does not constitute "personal information" under CCPA (Cal. Civ. Code § 1798.140(v)(1)) because it does not identify or reasonably link to a particular consumer.

Personal information about California residents held by dealers, insurers, and the DMV is subject to their existing CCPA obligations. ShieldVIN does not create new CCPA exposure.

---

## 6. Cross-Border Data Flows

Midnight Network is a distributed ledger. Nodes may be located in multiple jurisdictions. The on-chain data (which contains no PII, per Section 4.1) is replicated globally as part of normal validator operation.

Under GDPR Chapter V (transfers to third countries), this would be a significant compliance issue if the on-chain data contained personal data. Because it does not, Chapter V does not apply to the ledger data.

Off-chain PII flows between parties (e.g. a UK insurer querying a UK vehicle) remain subject to existing cross-border data transfer rules applicable to those parties.

## 7. Summary

| Regulation | Requirement | ShieldVIN Approach |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| GDPR Art. 17 | Right to erasure | No PII on-chain. Off-chain PII erased by the holding party. |
| GDPR Art. 25 | Data protection by design | Privacy-by-default architecture. ZK proofs enforce minimum disclosure. |
| GDPR Art. 5(1)(c) | Data minimisation | On-chain data reduced to minimum technically necessary. |
| GDPR Art. 35 | DPIA | A DPIA for the off-chain integration points (dealer systems, insurer APIs) is recommended at pilot stage. No DPIA required for the blockchain layer itself. |
| CCPA § 1798.100 | Right to know / delete | No CCPA-covered personal information on-chain. Off-chain data subject to holding party obligations. |
| GDPR Chapter V | Cross-border transfers | Not triggered. On-chain data is not personal data. |
